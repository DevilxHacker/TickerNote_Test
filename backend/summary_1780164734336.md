## Business Snapshot & Model
Ather Energy Limited (formerly Ather Energy Private Limited) is an Indian EV company specializing in electric two-wheelers (E2Ws) and their associated ecosystem, including proprietary software, charging infrastructure, and smart accessories, all designed and developed in-house. The company's business model emphasizes technology ownership, vertical integration, and a focus on quality and user experience.

Key aspects of their business model include:
*   **Vertically Integrated Approach:** Control over hardware and software design allows for rapid introduction of new technologies and management of quality factors like battery life, thermal management, durability, and safety. 80% of key hardware and 100% of the software stack are built internally.
*   **Software-Defined Ecosystem (AtherStack):** This ecosystem, comprising AtherStack software, charging infrastructure, and smart accessories, provides real-time insights into usage patterns and product performance, enabling faster updates and feature improvements. 88% of customers opt for AtherStack's advanced features.
*   **Premium Brand Positioning:** Products and services are built around quality, reliability, and attention to detail, commanding a premium price in their respective E2W segments.
*   **Scalable Technology Platforms:** The modular architecture of platforms like Ather 450, EL (new scooter platform), and Zenith (motorcycle platform) enables cost-efficient integration of new features and accelerates product development.
*   **Capital-Efficient, Flexible Operations:** The company outsources component manufacturing to allocate more capital to R&D and uses an asset-light distribution network through retail partners to minimize capital expenditures.

Ather Energy's product portfolio includes the Ather 450 range (450S, 450X LR, 450X HR, 450 Apex) and the Ather Rizta range (Rizta S, Rizta Z LR, Rizta Z HR), catering to both performance and convenience segments. The Ather Rizta, launched in FY25, targets the convenience segment with features like a large seat, WhatsApp notifications on the dashboard, Alexa Skills voice commands, and up to 56L of storage.

The company has developed India's largest E2W fast-charging network, Ather Grid, with 3,611 chargers across 360+ cities in India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka. All chargers use the LECCS (Light Electric Vehicle Combined Charging Standard), a BIS-recognized standard developed by Ather.

As of March 31, 2025, Ather Energy has 375 Experience Centres globally (351 in India) and 282 Service Centres globally (278 in India). The company's manufacturing facilities in Hosur, Tamil Nadu, have an installed capacity of 420,000 E2Ws and 379,800 battery packs per year. A new factory (Factory 3.0) is under development in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar, Maharashtra, with the first phase expected to commence production in FY27, adding 0.5 million E2Ws to the annual capacity. Upon completion of both phases, total installed capacity is projected to reach 1.42 million E2Ws per year.

## Chairman/Managing Director's Letter
The Chairman/Managing Director's letter highlights FY25 as a pivotal year for the Indian electric two-wheeler (E2W) market, with electric mobility becoming the default choice. Ather Energy, as an early mover, has played a significant role in this transformation. The company's strategy of engineering the entire product and platform from the bottom up, with a vertically integrated design philosophy, has been key to its success.

Key points from the letter:
*   **Market Leadership:** Ather Energy is emerging as a market leader in South India, garnering 22% sales in Q4 FY25 across Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, Goa, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana.
*   **Volume Growth:** Volumes grew by 42% year-on-year, from 109,577 units in FY24 to 155,394 units in FY25, driven by the launch of the Ather Rizta.
*   **Product Diversification:** The Ather Rizta, a family scooter launched in FY25, has boosted sales and expanded the company's presence into the convenience scooter segment.
*   **R&D Focus:** Ather has a strong internal R&D engine, with 46% of on-roll employees working in R&D as of March 31, 2025. This has led to industry-first features like touchscreen dashboards, connectivity software, fast charging, and smart helmets.
*   **Manufacturing Expansion:** A new factory (Factory 3.0) is being built in Maharashtra to support future growth and the development of new scooter and motorcycle products on the EL and Zenith platforms.
*   **Distribution Network:** Rapid expansion of the distribution network, particularly in non-south markets, is underway.
*   **Path to Profitability:** The company is focused on building a structurally sound and capital-efficient business, aiming to become a profitable, pan-India EV brand.
*   **Future Outlook:** The E2W market is expected to grow significantly, with scooters powering this growth. By FY31, an estimated 70-75% of scooters are projected to be electric. Ather is well-positioned to capitalize on this trend through portfolio diversification, manufacturing scale, and network expansion.

## Industry & Macro Overview
**Indian Economy:**
*   India's economy showed steady growth and stability in FY25, estimated to grow by 6.5% (Second Advanced Estimate from NSO), following 9.2% growth in the prior year.
*   Growth is attributed to sustained economic activity, strong manufacturing and services sectors, beneficial government policies, and domestic demand.
*   India is the world's fourth-largest economy by nominal GDP, with targets of $7 trillion by FY30 and $30 trillion by 2047, supported by infrastructure investments, reforms, and technological integration.
*   Anticipated growth for FY26 is 6.5%, with projections to become the world's third-largest economy by 2030.
*   Key drivers include demographic advantages, increasing capital investments, proactive government schemes, strong consumer demand, improved rural spending, and government emphasis on capital expenditure and fiscal prudence.
*   Initiatives like Make in India 2.0, ease of doing business reforms, and PLI schemes aim to enhance manufacturing and exports.

**Domestic Two-Wheeler Industry:**
*   India is one of the largest motorized two-wheeler markets globally, with domestic sales reaching ~20 million units in FY25.
*   The industry is poised for sustained growth, driven by positive micro/macroeconomic environments, strong rural demand, premiumization, new model launches, shrinking replacement cycles, and financing support.
*   Overall two-wheeler sales are projected to grow at ~7% CAGR, reaching 29-30 million units by FY31. In an optimistic scenario, this could reach 30-31 million units by FY31 (~8% CAGR).
*   **Electric Two-Wheeler (E2W) Market:**
    *   Expected to grow at ~41% CAGR over the next five years.
    *   E2W penetration reached 5.8% in FY25 (up from 5.1% in FY24).
    *   Scooters are leading electrification, with ~15.7% of scooters being electric in FY25, projected to reach as high as 70% by FY31.
    *   EV penetration in motorcycles is projected to be 10% by FY31.
    *   March 2025 saw 131K electric two-wheeler registrations.
    *   Total E2W sales volume for FY25 was 1.15 million units, a 22% rise from 0.94 million units in FY24.
*   **Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) Market:**
    *   Expected to see moderate growth of ~2% CAGR.
    *   ICE motorcycles grew 5% to 12.2 million units in FY25.
    *   ICE scooters grew 13% to 6 million units in FY25.
*   **Premiumization Trend:** The industry is witnessing a clear premiumization trend, with rising sales of 125cc motorcycles and scooters. Ather is positioned to capitalize on this due to design innovation, manufacturing quality, expanding distribution, and customer service.
*   **Key Growth Catalysts for E2W:** Superior Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) compared to ICE, narrowing acquisition cost gap, rising awareness, expanding charging infrastructure, increased financing support, larger EV product portfolios, expansion of EV manufacturing capacity, and continued government support.
