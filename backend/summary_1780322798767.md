## Business Snapshot & Model
One 97 Communications Limited (Paytm) is an Indian public company primarily engaged in providing payment and financial services, alongside marketing services. The company's core strategy is payment-led, leveraging its AI-powered omni-channel payment platform to acquire and engage users, which then facilitates the distribution of higher-margin financial and marketing services. Paytm aims to serve consumers, merchants, MSMEs, and enterprises in India, with an increasing focus on AI integration across its products and processes to enhance efficiency, reduce costs, and improve productivity. The company has also begun exploring international expansion opportunities for its technology-led merchant payments and financial services distribution model.

| **Distribution of Financial Services** | Merchant loans, Personal loans, Equity broking, Mutual fund distribution (SIPs, gold, other wealth products), Insurance | Consumers, Merchants |
| **Marketing Services** | Advertising, Travel ticketing, Credit card distribution, Deals and gift vouchers | Consumers, Merchants |

**Value Chain:**
Paytm's value chain starts with its omni-channel payment platform, which acts as the primary acquisition engine for both consumers and merchants. This platform enables merchants to accept various payment options and provides consumers with diverse transaction capabilities. The extensive user base and transaction data generated from payment services then allow Paytm to cross-sell higher-margin financial services (loans, insurance, wealth management) and marketing services (advertising, ticketing, deals) to both consumer and merchant segments. AI platforms like Paytm ARMS and Periscope are integrated throughout the operations to automate processes, enhance fraud detection, optimize pricing, and improve customer retention, creating a high-retention flywheel.

## Industry & Macro Overview
**Industry Environment and Demand Drivers:**
India is experiencing a rapid digital revolution, significantly impacting economic growth and living standards. The country leads globally in real-time payments volume, accounting for 48.5% in 2024. The digital economy is projected to contribute one-fifth of the national income by 2029-30, surpassing agriculture and manufacturing. India's resilient economic outlook, with real GDP growth of 6.5% in FY 2024-25 and a projected 6.5% expansion in FY 2025-26, provides a robust foundation for sustained domestic consumption. Accelerating digital adoption, rising disposable incomes, and a growing middle class (projected to reach 38% of the population by 2031) are driving substantial increases in consumer spending, forecasted to rise from $1.9 trillion in 2021 to nearly $5.2 trillion by 2031. Artificial Intelligence (AI) is identified as a frontier technology, expected to increase productivity by 2.6% in the organized sector and 2.8% in the unorganized sector by 2030, playing a crucial role in India's economic transformation.

The FinTech industry in India is the third-largest globally, with an 87% adoption rate, well above the global average of 64%. It is transitioning from disruption to collaborative transformation with traditional financial institutions. FinTechs contributed ~$20 billion (5% of BFSI sector revenue) in FY23, with revenues projected to scale to $200 billion (~20% share) by 2030. This growth is expected across key sectors:
- Digital lending book size: 6.6x growth from $45 billion in 2024 to $300 billion by 2030.
- WealthTech AUM: 6.6x increase from $35 billion to $237 billion.
- InsurTech GWP: Fourfold increase from $4 billion to $15 billion.

The payments ecosystem is becoming structurally viable with MDR on select instruments and subscription revenues.

## Management Discussion & Analysis Snapshot
In FY 2025, the Company focused on strengthening its core payments business and expanding high-margin financial and marketing services. Operating revenue (including UPI incentives) was INR 6,900 Cr, with a Contribution Profit of INR 3,678 Cr and a Contribution Margin of 53%. EBITDA was INR (1,507) Cr, impacted by Q1FY 2025 disruptions, but showed sequential improvement, reaching INR (88) Cr in Q4FY 2025. The Company achieved profitability in Q1FY 2026 with EBITDA of INR 72 Cr.

Key operational highlights include:
- Successful transition to a Third-Party Application Provider (TPAP) for UPI under a multibank model, leading to increased Average Monthly Transacting Users (MTU) from 7.0 Cr in Q3FY 2025 to 7.2 Cr in Q4FY 2025.
- Continued leadership in merchant payments with innovations like QR, Soundbox, and All-in-One POS. The merchant subscription base for devices grew to 1.24 Cr by March 2025.
- Significant growth in merchant loan distribution, increasing 24% YoY to INR 13,958 Cr, with over 50% distributed to repeat borrowers. The company is transitioning to a non-DLG model for disbursements with its largest lending partner.
- Monetization of non-core assets, including the PayPay stake and the movies and events business, generating over INR 2,000 Cr each, strengthening the balance sheet.
- AI-first approach integrated across operations, driving efficiency, fraud detection, and customer experience.
- Exploration of international expansion opportunities for its technology-led merchant payments and financial services distribution model in UAE, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, and Singapore, with results expected after 3 years.

The outlook remains focused on strengthening core payments, expanding high-margin loan distribution, and embedding AI-First approaches. The successful TPAP transition and new UPI user onboarding are expected to drive MTU growth and monetization.
