## Business Snapshot & Model
Ather Energy Limited is a pure-play EV company specializing in electric two-wheelers (E2Ws) and an associated product ecosystem. This includes proprietary software (AtherStack), charging infrastructure (Ather Grid), and smart accessories, all conceptualized, designed, and developed in-house in India. The company's core strategy revolves around technology ownership through vertical integration, controlling both hardware and software aspects of its products. Ather aims to deliver a superior mobility experience by integrating technology, sustainability, and performance. It addresses key barriers to EV adoption, such as charging anxiety and battery warranty, through its robust ecosystem. The company leverages its R&D capabilities, manufacturing facilities, and expanding distribution network to capitalize on the growing Indian E2W market, targeting both performance and convenience scooter segments.

**Value Chain**
Ather Energy employs a vertically integrated approach to designing EVs, controlling all key aspects of product design, hardware, and software.
- **R&D and Design:** In-house development of 80% of key hardware components (vehicle architecture, chassis, battery packs, BMS, VCU, motor controller, transmission, electronics, signal harness, fast charging system) and 100% of the software stack (AtherStack). This includes continuous investment in R&D facilities (IBC Knowledge Park, Proto Lab, Product Validation Lab) and a strong IP portfolio (203 registered designs, 47 registered patents, 313 pending patent applications).
- **Manufacturing:** State-of-the-art facilities in Hosur, Tamil Nadu, with an installed capacity of 420,000 E2Ws and 379,800 battery packs per year. Expansion planned with Factory 3.0 in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar, Maharashtra, to add 0.5 million E2Ws capacity in Phase 1 (FY27), aiming for a total of 1.42 million E2Ws/year. Manufacturing processes are integrated with MES and Industry 4.0 technologies.
- **Supply Chain:** Robust, diversified, and localized supplier base with 99% of E2W components procured through domestic vendors (by BOM value excluding lithium-ion cells) and 65% multi-sourced. Direct arrangements with technology players for selected components (lithium-ion cells, motors, semiconductors). Outsourcing component manufacturing to allocate capital to R&D.
- **Distribution & Sales:** Three-tier retail model (Flagship: 4,000+ Sq ft, Mid-size: 1,200+ Sq ft, Compact: 500+ Sq ft) through 375 Experience Centres across India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka (as of March 31, 2025). Asset-light approach leveraging retail partners and authorized distributors.
- **Service:** Digitally-enabled network of 282 Service Centres across India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka. Introduction of ExpressCare service for periodic maintenance within 60 minutes and Ather Gold Service Centres for enhanced experience.

## Industry & Macro Overview
- **Industry environment and demand drivers:**
  - The Indian two-wheeler (2W) industry is poised for sustained growth, projected at ~7% CAGR to 29-30 million units by FY31 (optimistic scenario: ~8% CAGR to 30-31 million units).
  - The electric two-wheeler (E2W) market is experiencing remarkable growth, projected at ~41% CAGR over the next five years, with E2W penetration anticipated to reach ~35% of overall 2W sales by FY31 (optimistic scenario: ~40%).
  - Scooters are expected to lead electrification in 2W, with E2W penetration in scooters projected to be as high as 70% in FY31 (from ~16% in FY25).
  - Demand drivers include: rising customer awareness, strong government support (PLI, Make in India), vastly superior Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for EVs, narrowing acquisition cost gap between EV and ICE, expanding charging infrastructure, increased financing support, larger EV product portfolios from leading players, and expansion of EV manufacturing capacity.
  - Premiumisation trend: growing demand for ≥125cc motorcycles and scooters, with 125cc scooters increasing market share from 47% in FY19 to 61.31% in FY25.
  - Macroeconomic improvements: India's economy exhibited steady growth and stability in FY25 (estimated 6.5% GDP growth), projected to grow at 6.5% in FY26, becoming the world's third-largest economy by 2030.
- **Key challenges and company's industry positioning:**
  - The E2W market saw fierce competition in FY25 with discounts and offerings below INR 1 lakh ex-showroom price.
  - Ather's premium positioning ensures revenue per scooter remained above INR 1 lakh (INR 128,295 for FY25), with software driving additional revenue (88% of customers purchased Propack, adding 6% to revenue).
  - Ather is positioned as an early mover and leader in the E2W transformation, with a vertically integrated model, strong R&D, diverse product line (performance and convenience scooters), and expanding distribution.
  - The company is capitalizing on the premiumization trend and the shift towards electrification.
