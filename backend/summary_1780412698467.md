## Business Snapshot & Model
Dabur India Limited is a global Ayurvedic and Natural Health Care company with a legacy of over 140 years. The company's core business revolves around providing health, wellness, and natural care products, deeply rooted in the science of Ayurveda and powered by innovation. Its diverse portfolio caters to evolving modern lifestyles while maintaining its heritage. Dabur aims to contemporize Ayurveda and make it relevant for new generations, dedicated to the health and wellbeing of every household.

| Category | Products/Services | Customer Segments |
|---|---|---|
| **Health & Wellness** | Ayurvedic and Natural Health Care products | Households across generations and geographies |
| **Hair Care** | Dabur Amla Hair Oil, Vatika Hair Oil | Consumers seeking natural hair care solutions |
| **Oral Care** | Dabur Red Toothpaste | Consumers seeking Ayurvedic toothpaste |
| **Beverages** | Real Juices, Real Nectar, Real Zero beverages, Carbonated Soft Drinks (CSD) | Consumers seeking fruit-based and fizzy beverages |
| **Home Care** | Odonil Air Freshener | Households for home hygiene and fragrance |
| **Other FMCG** | Shampoos, other personal care items | General consumers |

**Value Chain:**
The value chain involves sourcing agri-based raw materials and medicinal herbs, manufacturing in 12 locations across 9 countries, and distributing products to over 120 countries. The company emphasizes backward integration for herbs and honey, vendor diversification, and agile value chain realignment. Technology is a key enabler, with investments in IoT, AI-driven monitoring, RPA, and cloud migration for enhanced efficiency and decision-making.

## Industry & Macro Overview
**Industry environment and demand drivers:**
The global trade landscape faced uncertainty, but India demonstrated stability with a real GDP growth of 6.4% in FY 2024-25, close to its decadal average. This growth was supported by strong rural consumption, a favorable demographic dividend, and an expanding middle class. Rural India remained resilient, outpacing urban growth in FMCG consumption. The company anticipates a sequential recovery in consumption trends in 2025-26, driven by forecasts of a normal monsoon, improving macroeconomic indicators, sustained government investment in infrastructure, and easing inflation.

**Key challenges and company's industry positioning (factual disclosures only):**
- **Key Challenges:** Persistent food inflation impacting urban FMCG consumption. Evolving climate regulations and rising energy costs pose transition risks. Water scarcity and extreme weather events can disrupt supply chains and production. Tightening regulations under India's EPR and PWM Rules may increase compliance costs. Product classification under tax laws and stamp duty on past share issuances present financial and regulatory risks. Unethical marketing practices, cyberattacks, and critical raw material availability due to unorganized supply chains are also challenges.
- **Company's Industry Positioning:** Dabur is positioned as one of the world's largest Ayurvedic and Natural Health Care companies, with a strong legacy and trusted brand presence in over 120 countries. It holds significant market share across more than 90% of its portfolio. The company is proactively adapting to market shifts, including the rise of e-commerce and quick commerce, and is investing in sustainable practices and technology to maintain its competitive edge in the natural health and wellness space.

## Risks & Other Material Disclosures
**Critical Risks:**
*   **Financial and Regulatory Risks:** Ongoing risks related to product classification under tax laws, potentially leading to disputes, additional taxes, interest, and penalties. Mitigation involves legal proceedings, interim relief, and continued engagement with tax authorities.
*   **Compliance Risks:** Exposure to regulatory risks from evolving interpretations of legal requirements, including biodiversity-related regulations and stamp duty on past share issuances. Mitigation includes legal proceedings and financial provisions.
*   **Marketing Risk:** New draft regulations may reduce marketability for products with certain nutritional profiles, impacting consumer appeal and sales. Mitigation involves industry representation, inputs to FSSAI for exemptions, consumer studies, and product reformulation (e.g., reducing sugar in Real Nectar, launching Real Zero).
*   **Inflation Risk:** Rise in material costs leading to higher production costs and potential impact on profit margins and consumer purchasing behavior. Mitigation strategies include strategic buying, selective price increases, backward integration for raw materials, cost-saving initiatives, and import substitution.
*   **IT & Cybersecurity Risk:** Risks from digitalization and data-driven operations, including data privacy breaches and cyberattacks. Mitigation involves a Data Privacy Framework and Policy, cyber liability insurance, ongoing employee training, and strict access controls.
*   **Climate Risk:** Unpredictable weather patterns affecting demand for seasonal products and availability of critical herbs. Mitigation includes leveraging weather prediction tools, shifting focus to less season-dependent products/geographies, tighter demand forecasting, driving year-round relevance of seasonal products, increasing promotional activities, and increasing availability of critical herbs through own plantations, contractual farming, and alternative species.
*   **Reputational Risk:** Social media influence, misinformation, and public scrutiny impacting brand perception and sales. Mitigation involves maintaining high product quality, ensuring accuracy in claims and advertising, real-time social media monitoring, tailored response strategies, and regular reporting of reputational risks.

**Non-Critical Risks:**
*   **Critical Raw Material Availability Risk:** Challenges due to unorganised supply chain, lack of traceability, and regulatory scrutiny for medicinal plants and honey. Mitigation includes auction/RFQ processes for herb procurement, backward integration for herb sourcing, developing viable substitutions, and maintaining sufficient stock of honey.
*   **Single Vendor Sourcing Risk:** Dependency on single vendors for certain inputs, increasing exposure to supply disruptions, pricing volatility, and quality risks. Mitigation involves diversifying vendor base, developing alternate sources for packaging materials, and quarterly review processes for new vendor induction.
*   **Local Employment Policy Risks:** State-level industrial policies mandating local domicile employment ratios, limiting hiring flexibility and impacting productivity. Mitigation involves commitment to maintaining prescribed local domicile ratios (e.g., 80% in Jammu Units I & II, 72% in Unit V; 81% managerial, 93% non-managerial in Tezpur; 77% in Indore).
*   **Consumer and Reputational Risks:** Rising consumer activism around product quality, leading to complaints, social media posts, and regulatory checks. Mitigation includes timely refunds, monitoring honey crystallisation complaints, speedy replacements of complaint packs, and daily social listening.
*   **Operational Safety Risks:** Fire incidents at manufacturing facilities. Mitigation includes insurance, fire safety audits, implementation of findings, follow-up audits, fire NOCs, regular training, and recognition programs for safety responses.
*   **Counterfeit and Market Credibility Risks:** Presence of spurious and look-alike products, particularly in rural and wholesale markets. Mitigation involves legal action, criminal raids, and a structured "Break The Fake" program with rewards for reporting valid cases.
*   **Water Availability Risks:** Manufacturing units in high water-stressed regions, vulnerable to scarcity. Mitigation includes multi-locational manufacturing footprint, strategic manufacturing expansion, 30% reduction in water intensity, and progressing towards water positivity by 2030.

**Other Material Disclosures:**
*   Climate, water, and forest-related risks and opportunities are incorporated into the Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) framework.
*   Internal and external audits of risk management and compliance processes are ongoing.
*   Robust and multi-layered Business Continuity Plan (BCP) implemented across all manufacturing units, corporate offices, and supply chain operations.
*   Diversified supplier network and identification of alternate sources for essential ingredients.
*   Disaster recovery testing for critical IT systems.
*   Periodic simulation exercises and readiness audits for BCM effectiveness.
